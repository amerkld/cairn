# Shipping Solo

*A practical book for people building and shipping software alone.*

Not a memoir. A handbook — decisions you have to make, ordered roughly by when you have to make them.

## Working outline

1. The setup — opinions on tools so you stop deciding and start building
2. Scope — what to build first, what to refuse
3. Money — pricing, payment stack, taxes (lightly)
4. Ops — status, email, analytics, the boring stuff that can kill you
5. Launch — low-stakes launches that compound
6. Aftermath — the first 90 days, and what to do when the graph goes flat

## Constraints I want to keep

- Every chapter ends with a one-page checklist.
- No more than 40,000 words total.
- Code examples run *as-is*.

## Why not yet

Writing a book while launching a product is the definition of doing neither well. Revisit post-Waypoint v1.
