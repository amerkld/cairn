> A system is a set of things — people, cells, molecules, or whatever — interconnected in such a way that they produce their own pattern of behavior over time.

What stuck:

1. Stocks change slowly; flows can change fast. Most mistakes in planning come from confusing the two.
2. Delays are the dominant source of oscillation. If feedback lags, you overshoot. Every time.
3. The structure of a system produces its behavior — blaming people is almost always the wrong frame.

Things to sit with:
- Where am I treating a ~~~person~~~ as the variable when it's actually the *structure*?
- What loops in Waypoint's growth funnel have delays I'm not modelling?
