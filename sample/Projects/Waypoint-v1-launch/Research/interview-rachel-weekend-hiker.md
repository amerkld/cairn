# R., 41, weekend hiker

Interviewed 2026-03-27 via one 45-min call. 4 months in beta. Lives in Utah, hikes mostly Wasatch and Uintas.

## The signal

R. isn't a through-hiker. She plans one day-hike most weekends. Her whole mental model is different from J.'s:

- Doesn't obsess over elevation profiles
- Uses the app *before* the hike, on the couch, to pick where to go
- Offline mode matters because she hikes slot canyons — signal drops completely for hours

## What she loves

> "I just want the map to work when I'm in a canyon with one bar. That's it. That's the whole ask."

## Friction points

- The "saved routes" list is chronological; she'd rather group by area/region.
- First-time setup asked her to pick a unit system three times. "Is it miles or not?"
- No printable view. She sometimes wants a paper backup.

## Takeaways

Two segments are starting to emerge clearly: **thru-hikers** (high feature density, technical) and **weekenders** (simple, reliable). Messaging should avoid the words "thru-hiking" in the hero line — they'd self-select out.
