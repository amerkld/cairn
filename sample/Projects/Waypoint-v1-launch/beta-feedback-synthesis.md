# What beta users actually said

Three rounds of interviews + the in-app feedback widget. Patterns, not individual quotes, are what matter.

## Patterns (by mention count)

| Theme                            | Mentions | Action                                  |
| -------------------------------- | -------- | --------------------------------------- |
| "I want this offline"            | 28       | *Done.* Shipped Apr 18.                 |
| Elevation profile is too narrow  | 14       | Planned — post-launch.                  |
| Trip-sharing via link            | 11       | In progress, shipping before launch.    |
| Apple Watch companion            | 9        | Someday. Not v1.                        |
| "Can I import from Strava?"      | 7        | Discussed & deferred. OAuth is painful. |
| Print-friendly route cards       | 4        | Nice-to-have. Skipping v1.              |

## Standout quotes

> "This is the first trail app that doesn't feel like it was designed in a conference room."
> — J., thru-hiker, beta interview #12

> "I just want the map to work when I'm in a canyon with one bar. That's it. That's the whole ask."
> — R., weekend hiker, beta interview #19

## Synthesis

The center of gravity is *reliability in the wild*, not *more features at the desk*. Every v1 launch message should make that case. The Apple Watch crowd is real but small; we can come back to it.
